The retail landscape is evolving as ecommerce continues to prosper as the prime site for consumer sales. Because the benefits of launching and upkeeping an online store outweigh the high startup costs, monthly lease payments, design fees, and labor associated with brick and mortar, many local retailers are retreating to the ecommerce space. The average store costs around $100,000 and takes more than 60 days to become operational. However, offline channels drive roughly 94% of all retail sales, and many online retailers yearn for public visibility.
AISLED provides the benefits of both online and offline commerce. Our company delivers quality storefront displays merging the physical shopping experience with ecommerce. This mixed reality platform provides local retailers and craftsmen the benefits of a brick and mortar storefront, i.e. physical presence and brand visibility, while simultaneously maintaining low operation costs. Our storefronts cost less than 1% of the traditional brick and mortar shop and only takes a small fraction of the time to install. Our aim is to make online shopping more accessible to the local masses.

The Retail Landscape is Evolving

Storefront vacancies are at an all-time high as many brick and mortar retailers have succumb to their online competitors. The vacancy rate in Boston is 10%, while areas in New York City have rates as high as 20%. Alarmingly, these rates are projected to rise. Retail sales are thriving, just not in urban space. These unoccupied storefronts have a profound impact on communities, their residents, landlords, and developers.
